State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) BMR Scheme for Rice Husking Mills in Sindh

SEDF, in collaboration with State Bank of Pakistan & Participating Financial Institutions (PFIs) and Rice Millers’ Association, devised the BMR scheme for the upgradation of Rice Husking Mills in Sindh. The scheme aims to address issues such as:

  • Out-dated/obsolete technology
  • Operational losses as high as 40%
  • High Energy Costs

SEDF’s Objective

To Modernize and Revamp existing Rice Husking Units and Establish New ones with operationally efficient units throughout Sindh.

Incentive Offered

Maximum size of loan granted under the Scheme is PKR 30 million (additional loan of PKR 8 million can be acquired for mechanized paddy dryer).

  • Credit Guarantee of 30%
  • Subsidy on Refinance Rate beyond 2% (rice miller pays 2% only)

Benefits of the Scheme

  • Profitability - 15%
  • Export Quality Rice
  • Capacity – 30%
  • Energy Efficient
  • Market Access
  • Employment Generation
  • Value Addition

Project Name: Rice International (Pvt.) Limited

Owner(s): Mr. Farhan Azeem Siddiqui

Project Location: Late Stop, Thakani Stop, Mirpur Sakro, District Thatta

Capacity: 10 tons / hour

Number of Direct Employment Generated (indirect): 20 (30)

Project Size: PKR 15 million (total project value PKR 200 million)

Equity: PKR 05 millions

Bank Loan: PKR 10 millions

Debt to Equity Ratio: 29:71

Bank: Habib Bank Limited

Project Overview

Rice International (Pvt.) Limited procured automatic rice bagging machine, conveyor belt, stand with up & down mechanism for sewing machine, and electric control panels.

SEDF’s Assistance Impact

SEDF has provided an assistance of PKR 10 million to Rice International (Pvt.) Limited, resulting in improved efficiency and productivity of the mill.

Project Name: Ali Rice Mill

Owner(s): Mr. Abdul Razzaque Khowaja

Project Location: Ver Pir Patha, District Thatta

Number of Direct Employment Generated (indirect): 10 (15)

Capacity: 7 tons / hour

Project Size: PKR 26.76 million

Equity: PKR 16.76 million

Bank Loan: PKR 10 million

Debt to Equity Ratio: 37:63

Bank: Soneri Bank Limited

Project Overview

Ali Ali Rice Mill had obsolete and outdated machinery and equipment installed with no fire alarm system and ordinary electrical wiring that caused a fire incident. In result, the entire mill was burnt. Ali Ali Rice Mill has availed the BMR Scheme to upgrade the mill with improved equipment and machinery and safety systems.

SEDF’s Assistance Impact

SEDF has provided an assistance of PKR 10 million to Ali Ali Rice Mill. The project has elevators, paddy separators, and silky polishers installed that improved the production.

Project Name: Anmol Rice Industries

Owner(s): Mr. Doulat Ram and Mr. Mohan Mal

Project Location: Survey No. 95/1 (part) & 96/2 (part), Deh Kahdan, Ali Bunder Road, Badin District

Number of Direct Employment Generated (indirect): 10 (20)

Capacity: 5 tons / hour

Project Size: PKR 80 million

Equity: PKR 70 million

Bank Loan: PKR 10 million

Debt to Equity Ratio: 13:87

Bank: National Bank of Pakistan

Project Overview

Anmol Rice Mill is a new husking unit with a capacity of 5 tons/hour consisting of local as well as Chinese machinery. The equipment and machinery are installed, and the mill is operational.

SEDF’s Assistance Impact

SEDF has provided an assistance of PKR 10 million to Anmol Rice Mill. This mill is able to produce quality grain at 5 tons/hour. The unit minimized the grain breakup ratio, and the average grain length has been increased up to 6.8 mm. Overall impact on revenue has also increased i.e., 15-18% annually.

Joint Venture between Growers and Processors/Exporters

SEDF, in association with Garib Sons (Pvt.) Limited, has introduced a partnership between Rice Growers and Processors/Exporters in the Rice value chain.

Twofold Benefits:

  • Premium Price to Farmers for quality output (exclusion of middleman)
  • Quality Output for the Processors/exporter

Salient Features:

  • Extension Services Provided by the Exporter (Garib Sons Pvt. Limited)
  • Guaranteed Off-take of the Produce of the farmers
  • Financial inclusion of the farmer into processing (value chain)
  • Growers become partners in Profits
  • Enhanced quality and output
