Kambar Shahdadkot

History

This district was a part of the region ruled by Kalhoros and Talpurs. Both these dynasties are the decedents of Abbasids. They arrived in Sindh during the invasion of Nadir Shah. According to historical records, Shahdadkot was founded around 1713. It was a major town on the route between Larkana and Gandawah. Kalhoro dynasty ruled this area from 1700 to 1783 and the Talpur dynasty ruled from 1783-1843. After independence, Kamber and Shahdadkot both remained talukas of District Larkana. In 2005, the government of Pakistan bifurcated Larkana forming a new district called Kamber Shahdadkot, including the towns of Kambar Khan and Shahdadkot.
