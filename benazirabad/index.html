Shaheed Benazirabad

History

Historically, this region was ruled by different dynasties including the Soomra (1024-1351), Summa (1335¬1520), Arghun (1520-1650), Kalhora (1657-1783) and Talpur (1783-1843). When Britain invaded the subcontinent, General Charles Napier, a commander in the British Army, defeated the Talpur dynasty and conquered Sindh in 1843. British named this taluka after the name of a migrator, Syed Nawab Shah, which was initially called Nawabshah town, and then developed as Nawabshah taluka and finally it was upgraded to the status of Nawabshah district in 1912.
