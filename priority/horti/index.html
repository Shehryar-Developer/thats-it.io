Horticulture & Floriculture

Cluster Facts

  • Pakistan is predominantly agrarian society, among top ten producers of fresh fruits & vegetables in the world.
  • Agriculture contributes 20% to the GDP of Pakistan and provides 35% of employment to our labor force yet per acre productivity is among the lowest in the world.
  • Unfortunately 25% - 40% of agri-produce is wasted due to post harvest handling, lack of processing and storage facilities
  • Most of the fruits are consumed fresh, while only 3% - 5% of fruits undergo processing for value-added derivatives, out of which only 5% is exported.
  • Establishment of quality processing units and storage facilities will enhance value of horticulture produce and help utilize the export potential.

Bloom Agritech (Pvt.) Limited

Proprietor Mr. Ahmed Nini
Nature of the Project Modern Hydroponic Farm
Project Location Nooriabad
Direct Employment Generated (indirect) 10 (20)
Project Size PKR 120 million
Equity PKR 50 million
Bank Loan PKR 70 million
Debt to Equity Ratio 58:42
Bank Bank Al Falah Limited

Project Overview

Depleting water resources call for innovative solutions for the future of the agri-sector. Hydroponics is a modern farming technique that enables farmers to cultivate off-season fruits and vegetables with a lesser amount of land and water resources, making efficient use of scarce agri-resources.

The Bloom Agritech (Pvt.) Limited will revolutionize the traditional agriculture model in Pakistan. The approach could be broken down into two key areas:

  1. Bloom is set up a hydroponic greenhouse facility to provide off-season vegetables to the local market.
  2. Bloom will engage local farmers to buy their produce, grade and package it for both local and export markets. The greenhouse facility will be built in Nooriabad, Sindh.

Bloom’s greenhouse is equipped with an automated climate control system which ensures that regardless of the weather patterns, it will maintain the optimum temperature and humidity across the greenhouse.

These systems are also connected to the cloud, which will ensure that all the data is logged onto a remote system, as well as provide visibility into the farm from anywhere. Bloom has hired services of a UAE-based greenhouse contracting company that has vast experience in building greenhouses in the U.A.E. Climate control system has been purchased from a leasing company based in California, while dosing systems are sourced from New Zealand.

SEDF’s Assistance Impact: Bloom has also set up a delivery center within the city of Karachi and launched online operations to serve smaller customers once the production is established. By going direct to the consumer, the company has ensured that they provide the customers hygienic and fresh fruits and vegetables.

Al-Rahim Agri Processing (Pvt.) Limited

Director(s): Mr. Anas Kapadia, Mustafa Hanif, Mr. Mukhtiar Ali Sheikh, and Haji Haroon Kapadia

Nature of the Project: Multi-Fruits Processing Plant (Hot Water Treatment Plant/Vapour Treatment Plant and Cold Store Facility will soon be included)

Project Location: Khairpur Special Economic Zone (KSEZ), Khairpur

Direct Employment Generated (indirect): 40 (80)

Annual Capacity: 15,000 tons

Project Size: PKR 490 million

Equity: PKR 170 million

Bank Loan: PKR 320 million

Debt to Equity Ratio: 65:35

Bank: Bank Al Habib Limited

Project Overview

Unfortunately, due to improper post-harvest handling and lack of required infrastructure (processing and cold storages), 30% - 40% of our fruits are being wasted. Most of the fruits are consumed fresh, only 3% - 5% of fruits undergo value-added processing, and only about 5% out of it is being exported, which is far below the potential of this sector.

Sindh, being the second-largest fruit-producing province of Pakistan, requires quality pulping facilities to process and add-value to its agri-produce to satisfy the increased demand for pulp/concentrate/nectar and puree by utilizing locally grown fruits. Inculcating efficiencies in the value chain of Guava, Mango, Tomato, and other fruits and vegetables will lead towards the reduction in post-harvest losses, increased employment opportunities, and a maximized crop value for the farmers.

The project is the first of its kind in central/upper Sindh region. The project processes and preserves the fruits available locally in substantial quantities to produce aseptically packed/frozen purees/clear juice concentrates/tomato paste and products for growing demand in international and local markets.

SEDF’s Assistance Impact

SEDF has provided financial assistance (mark-up subsidy) for a state-of-the-art multi-fruit processing facility at Pakistan’s first agri-processing zone (KSEZ).

Syed Agriculture Farm

Proprietor: Mr. Qadir Bux
Nature of the Project: Maize based silage for livestock
Project Location: Saeedabad
Direct Employment Generated (indirect): 35 (100)
Project Size: PKR 35 million
Equity: PKR 15 million
Bank Loan: PKR 20 million
Debt to Equity Ratio: 60:40
Bank: Bank Al Falah Limited

Project Overview

Syed Agriculture Farm is a sole proprietorship concern established by Mr. Qadir Bux. He is a resident of Bhan Saeedabad, District Jamshoro, and has been involved in Agri-business for more than 25 years. The main crops cultivated are Maize, Wheat, and Cotton.

He started cultivating Maize for Silage making purposes, initially dedicating 20 acres of land for silage. However, due to high demand for silage in the local market, it was later increased to 250 acres. Currently, he is renting out machinery for silage, which reduces his profitability margins. Hence, he has decided to buy his own machinery on finance to improve his profit margins. Syed Agri Farm produces various sizes of silage bales (65kg. - 1,000kg) based on customer demand and sells it in the local as well as Karachi market.

SEDF’s Assistance Impact

SEDF has provided financial assistance (mark-up subsidy) for a Modern Harvester and Baler for Maize cultivation, which is to be used for making silage for livestock.
