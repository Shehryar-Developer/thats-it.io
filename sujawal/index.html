History

Hyderabad is the second largest city of Sindh Province. Founded in 1768 on the site of the ancient town of Noreen Kot by Ghulam Shah Kalhora, the saintly ruler of Sind, it was named for the Prophet Muhammad’s son-in-law, Ali, also known as Haidar. It remained the capital of Sind under the Talpur rulers, who succeeded the Kalhoras, until 1843 when, after the nearby battles of Miani and Dabo, it surrendered to the British and the capital was transferred to Karachi. The Hyderabad city is rich in culture, traditions and history due to the fact that it lies alongside the River Indus.
